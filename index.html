Lawrence Moore's Bio


I started taking classes at University of South Carolina-Aiken when I just 12, begining with calculus. I continued there as a concurrent student in highschool, taking courses from organic and physical chemistry to abstract algebra and analysis. Since then, I've studied CS at Georgia Tech and specialized in theory and artificial intelligence.

I've worked on developing 3D simulations for NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) to test the behavior of autonomous drones in the summer 2015. I also did a stint at Google, working on a classification problem on an AdWords team. I'm currently performing research to see if mosquitos can be detected when flying around in a video using deep learning. The hope is that once we can detect them, we'll be able to use flying drones to track and eliminate them. In the course of these projects, I've worked with Python, Java, Tensorflow, JavaScript, C++, Django, NumPy, OpenCV, and other languages. I've also become familiar with the state of the art in computer vision techniques along with a working knowledge of statistics and machine learning.

Outside of classes, I have a somewhat eclectic group of hobbies. I've played classical piano since I was 7, and had the opportunity to perform with the Augusta Symphony and win a few major statewide competitions in my homestate of South Carolina. I also served as the president of the Classical Music Network at GT for a year. I find teaching one of the most rewarding things to do on the side, and I have served as a TA for multiple courses at Georgia Tech and was a tutor for organic chemistry back in the day. I'm a former nationally ranked swimmer. If I have any time left, I'll usually end the day by reading philosophy.
